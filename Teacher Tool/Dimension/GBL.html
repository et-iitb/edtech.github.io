Student agency Check if the product provides adequate learner agency, meaning it allows students to take charge of their learning experiences, set their own goals, make decisions about their learning paths, and reflect on their progress.

Look at: Title of the AV content.

Check whether:
  • It is possible to set or reset their learning goals, choose which skills they want to practice as game activities, which topic or challenges to attempt in what sequence and so on.
  • Immersive game or role-playing game: learners can choose their role i.e. have control over their avatars (in terms of appearance, actions etc.). Learners can select a role from pre-created roles or create a role based on a template.
  • Tutorial based game or drill-and-practice games: there is scope to practice more than once and the same type of skills are getting assessed.
Social Learning Check if the product provides opportunities for learners to achieve their learning goals through social interaction?

Check whether: The game should provide opportunities for social learning in one or more of the ways listed below:
  • Collaboration: Learners may converse with other learners to achieve a common goal.
  • Competition: Synchronous (where an individual plays against other individual players) or, asynchronous (where an individual competes against previously recorded performance of peers) competition to help learners master the skill
  • Team-based play: Individuals form (small) groups to compete with other groups. Within a team, team members have to collaborate and cooperate to play.
  • Promotes anonymity during interaction in the game
